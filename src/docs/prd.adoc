[abstract]
ultrathink.call is a sovereign, server-less video call: four peers meet in a
browser mesh with screen sharing, no backend to operate and no login to build.
The one decision that shapes everything is to stay infrastructure-free — it
buys independence from US providers but caps the mesh at a handful of peers, so
the 20-person training case becomes the central open tension. This PRD fixes
the _why_ and the _what_; the Spec and the arc42 architecture carry the _how_.

== European trainers depend on US video tools

Trainers and coaches in Europe depend on US video services such as Zoom. Those
who want independence find gaps in the obvious alternatives today:

* *alfaview* did not throttle the bitrate under CPU load (heat); instead the
  presenter's video dropped out entirely. A dropout instead of graceful
  degradation is unacceptable in a training.
* *Jitsi Meet* feels janky in use, and the virtual background is too weak. In a
  training especially, participant privacy — their home surroundings on camera —
  must be protected.

The trainer has basic WebRTC knowledge but little experience, and wants to run
no servers and build no login. The walking skeleton has already proven that a
simple server-less P2P call is feasible in a static page. A product should grow
from it — in thin slices.

== Sovereignty, no infrastructure, no login drive the goals

=== Goals

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Goal
| G-1 | *Digital sovereignty.* No runtime component depends on a US company —
        not even STUN or signaling.
| G-2 | *No own infrastructure.* No backend, no servers to operate or patch.
        Delivered as a static page.
| G-3 | *No login.* No accounts, no authentication to build or maintain.
        (Derived constraint of G-2: a login would need a backend.)
| G-4 | *Stable under load.* Video degrades in a controlled way
        (resolution/bitrate) instead of dropping — the alfaview failure must not
        recur.
| G-5 | *Participant privacy.* Client-side background blur or replacement of
        good quality, applied before the frame is encoded.
| G-6 | *Modularity.* Signaling, STUN/ICE, and video processing sit behind ports
        and are swappable — protection against new lock-in.
| G-7 | *Incremental growth.* Thin slices: start at 2, then 4, vision up to ~20
        for trainings.
| G-8 | *Confidentiality by default.* All media and data channels are
        DTLS-SRTP encrypted; there is no unencrypted fallback.
| G-9 | *Data protection.* P2P with no backend means no controller-side storage;
        no participant PII is persisted or sent to any server, and camera/mic
        capture is consented.
|===

=== Non-Goals (for now)

* Account or permission system, federated identities.
* Cloud recording, server-side storage.
* Native mobile apps.
* More than ~20 participants; interop with SIP/Zoom/Teams.
* An SFU operated by us — but R-1 may reopen this for the training case.

[cols="1,1,3",options="header"]

| P-1 | Trainer / Host | Gives trainings, convenes the weekly round. Solid tech
        knowledge, low appetite for operations. Wants control and sovereignty.
        Acts as host only in trainings; in the weekly round there is no host.
| P-2 | Training Participant | Up to ~20 people, joining to learn. Want minimal
        effort and their surroundings on camera protected.

== Eight measurable criteria decide success

Measurable, not phrased as adjectives.

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Criterion
| SM-1 | The weekly 4-person round runs entirely on ultrathink.call for 4
         consecutive weeks, with no fallback to Zoom.
| SM-2 | An audit of every runtime endpoint — STUN, signaling broker, static
         host/CDN, DNS, and any third-party script or WASM model origin — shows
         no operator under US jurisdiction (corporate ownership or operating
         entity), checked against a named allowlist.
| SM-3 | Under sustained CPU saturation (≥ 90 % for ≥ 120 s) the sender steps
         resolution down through defined tiers but the stream stays connected at
         ≥ 10 fps — the alfaview dropout does not reproduce.
| SM-4 | With background blur on, a reviewer cannot read text or identify objects
         in the background region in ≥ 9 of 10 sampled frames at 720p.
| SM-5 | Joining a call takes < 60 s from opening a shared link — no install, no
         login.
| SM-6 | Every room ID is a CSPRNG-generated UUIDv4 (≥ 122 bits of entropy),
         never sequential or time-derived.
| SM-7 | Every media and data channel negotiates DTLS-SRTP; a connection that
         cannot encrypt does not fall back to plaintext, it fails.
| SM-8 | No participant PII (audio, video, IP, display name) is persisted by or
         transmitted to any server we or a third party operate; a network audit
         shows only peer-to-peer media and the minimal signaling/STUN exchange.
|===

== Two strategic decisions shape the build

=== Build, not adopt (build-vs-buy)

Jitsi Meet is open source (Apache-2.0); EU-self-hosted it _would_ be sovereign.
But self-hosting violates G-2 (infrastructure) and would carry the rejected
janky UX and weak background. Decision: rethink the solution as server-less P2P,
do not fork Jitsi. Rationale and alternatives belong in an arc42 ADR.

=== Access without login: a capability-URL distributed via `mailto:`

An email magic-link would need a sending and validation service — infrastructure
(G-2) and a piece of security (G-3). Instead: a *capability-URL*
`https://…/#room=<uuid>`. The UUID in the URL fragment _is_ the secret; whoever
holds the link may enter. The room ID is a CSPRNG-generated UUIDv4 (SM-6), so it
is not guessable or enumerable. No backend, no login. The URL fragment never
leaves the browser, so it does not land in server logs.

At R1 there is no broker yet, so the capability-URL carries the WebRTC SDP
itself in the fragment (`#invite=`/`#answer=`): the whole token is the bearer
secret, and possessing it — not guessing the room id — is what admits a peer.
Once the R2 broker exists, the `#room=<uuid>` form becomes the lookup key the
broker matches peers on, and the UUID is the secret. The CSPRNG room id (SM-6)
applies in both forms.

Distribution stays loosely coupled and infrastructure-free: the host opens the
call, and the app generates a `mailto:` link (subject plus body carrying the
capability-URL) that the host's *own* mail client sends to the other
participants. We send no mail ourselves; we only hand the user a pre-filled
draft. The same link can equally be shared over any other channel (RCS,
messenger). The security model and residual risks (R-3, R-9) are settled by the
arc42 architecture (threat model 8.1).

== The 20-participant tension is the central risk

Risks separate from technical debt; each carries probability/impact via a
priority and a mitigation hook for the later threat model (T-IDs in chapter 8.1).

[cols="1,1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Priority | Description and handling
| R-1 | high | *20 participants vs. mesh physics.* In a full mesh every peer
        uploads `N-1` streams; ~20 exceeds home uplinks. The remedy, an SFU,
        _is_ infrastructure (violates G-2). Tension between G-1/G-2 and G-7@20.
        Options for later ADRs: (a) asymmetric broadcast topology (only the
        trainer sends video, listeners audio-only if needed), (b) reconsider the
        SFU non-goal with a self-operated EU SFU (sovereign but infrastructure),
        (c) set a realistic maximum < 20. Decision deferred; does not block R0–R4.
| R-2 | medium | *Availability/stability of sovereign STUN/broker*, plus broker
        DoS, room squatting/spam, and malformed signaling. Mitigation: several
        swappable EU/community endpoints (G-6); validate inbound signaling.
| R-3 | medium | *Capability-URL leak, replay, and no revocation* → uninvited
        join (no auth). The link is a bearer secret; once shared it cannot be
        revoked without changing the room. Later mitigation: rotate room ID,
        host admit (see R-9).
| R-4 | medium | *Background-blur quality/performance* in the browser must beat
        Jitsi, yet costs CPU — conflicts with G-4 on weak devices. On model-load
        failure the camera fails closed (no raw frame transmitted).
| R-5 | low | *Browser only*, no native mobile experience.
| R-6 | medium | *IP/metadata disclosure.* STUN reveals each public IP, the
        broker sees connection metadata, and in a mesh every peer learns every
        other peer's IP. Orthogonal to jurisdictional sovereignty. Mitigation
        hook: optional TURN relay to hide IPs for at-risk participants (note:
        TURN reintroduces infrastructure — weigh against G-2).
| R-7 | high | *Untrusted signaling broker (MITM).* A malicious or compromised
        broker can tamper with SDP/ICE or attempt a man-in-the-middle on setup.
        Mitigation hook: verify DTLS certificate fingerprints out-of-band or via
        the capability channel.
| R-8 | medium | *Screen-share oversharing.* Sharing a whole screen leaks
        notifications, other windows, and tabs. Mitigation hook: prefer
        single-window/tab share and a pre-share warning.
| R-9 | medium | *Disruptive or gate-crashing participant.* A leaked/forwarded
        link lets uninvited or disruptive parties join; with no host admit/kick/
        mute a single bad actor can wreck a training. Host moderation is raised
        to Should scope.
| R-10 | medium | *Supply-chain/static-host integrity.* The product is a static
        page plus client-side JS/WASM (blur model); a compromised host, CDN, or
        dependency can defeat every client-side privacy property. Mitigation
        hook: Subresource Integrity, pinned dependencies, CSP, Trusted Types.
|===

== Open questions are handed off by role

*Product Owner*

* R5/training: is audio-only acceptable for listeners if that is what makes ~20
  feasible at all? Or must every listener send video?
* Does local recording belong in scope later? If so it requires explicit
  all-party recording consent (legally required in much of the EU) and is out of
  scope until that consent flow is specified.

*Architect*

* R-1: which topology for ~20 — asymmetric broadcast vs. own EU SFU? Decides
  compatibility with G-2.
* Which sovereign STUN/signaling broker (EU/community) becomes the default
  (R-2, D-9)?
* R-6: is a TURN relay offered for IP privacy, accepting the infrastructure cost?

*Developer*

* Which browser API for background segmentation (e.g. Insertable Streams + WASM
  model) satisfies G-5 without violating G-4?

*Domain Expert (Trainer)*

* What is the realistic upper bound of real training groups — 12, 16, 20?

*Operations*

* No open questions — operations are explicitly excluded by G-2 (static
  delivery).
